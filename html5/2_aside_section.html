Pav Bhaji Recipe

Your guide to making delicious Pav Bhaji at home

Ingredients

  • 4 Pav (bread rolls)
  • 2 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (peas, carrots, cauliflower), boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 1 capsicum, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tsp pav bhaji masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Lemon wedges, chopped onions, and coriander for garnish

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Vegetables

Boil the potatoes and mixed vegetables until they are soft. Once done, mash them thoroughly and set aside.

2. Cook the Bhaji

In a large pan, heat butter. Add the chopped onions and sauté until golden brown. Then, add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute. Next, add chopped tomatoes and capsicum. Cook until the tomatoes become soft.

Add pav bhaji masala, red chili powder, and salt. Mix well. Now, add the mashed vegetables and potatoes. Combine everything together and cook for 10-15 minutes on medium heat, stirring occasionally.

3. Toast the Pav

Slice the pav in half and toast them on a hot pan with butter until they are golden and crispy.

4. Serve

Garnish the bhaji with a dollop of butter, chopped onions, coriander, and a squeeze of lemon. Serve hot with the toasted pav.

Indian Curry Recipe

Your guide to making a flavorful Indian curry at home

Ingredients

  • 500g chicken (or vegetables for a vegetarian version)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 3 tomatoes, pureed
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 2 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
  • Rice or naan for serving

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Prepare the Base

In a large pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds. Then, add the finely chopped onions and sauté until golden brown.

2. Add Spices and Tomatoes

Add the ginger-garlic paste to the onions and sauté for another minute. Then, add the pureed tomatoes, coriander powder, turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt. Cook until the oil starts to separate from the mixture.

3. Cook the Chicken

Add the chicken pieces to the pan and mix well, ensuring that they are coated with the masala. Cover the pan and let it cook for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through.

4. Add Yogurt and Garam Masala

Lower the heat and stir in the yogurt slowly to avoid curdling. Cook for another 5 minutes. Finally, sprinkle garam masala over the curry and mix well.

5. Serve

Garnish the curry with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with steamed rice or naan.
